VR

Virtual Reality (VR) is a computer-generated simulation of a three-dimensional environment that can be interacted with in a seemingly real way, usually by wearing a headset that tracks the user's movements and displays a corresponding view on a screen in front of their eyes.

AR

Augmented Reality (AR) is often used in VR headsets to enhance the user experience by overlaying virtual objects on the real world. AR can be used to display information, guides, or additional elements to the user's view of the real world.

Vr's past: the Google Glass

The Google Glass were mixed reality glasses that were ahead of its time. Glasses that told the time, could take pictures, access the internet, use google maps, all through voice control. They became available to the public in May of 2014, but unfortunately flopped. Why weren't they successful? Many reasons. First of all, they did not solve any problems that consumers had. They were marketed as a hands-free smartphone, but had significantly less features than smartphones had. Secondly, they were sold at the steep price of $1,500 dollars. Lastly, they did not look appealing as many people thought they looked nerdy. The ease of having a smartphone with less features without having to use your hands was not worth the price point, and the Google Glass failed.

VR's future: META

Meta’s most recent headset, the Meta Quest Pro(right), is one of the most, if not the most, technologically advanced headsets to date. At $1,500 USD it is a headset that is targetted towards VR enthusiasts and workers who might need a VR for their job. Users can use it as a VR headset and play games on it and explore the metaverse. It is also a mixed reality headset, so users can interact with their surroundings while using it. This feature will be very useful in the future as it will make many industries more efficient. Engineers will be able to with the look and build of their creation. Some companies such as BMW and Jaguar are already using VR’s to hold early design and engineering reviews. Interior designers will have an easier job as they will be able to experiment with the design without having to move any real furniture. VR is even becoming useful in the healthcare industry as surgeons can train for surgery using it. The future of VR is promising, and as headsets become more affordable and less bulky, we will start seeing more and more headsets in our everyday life.
